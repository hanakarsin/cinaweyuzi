Nakuru Man in Mourning after Losing Campus Lover He Wedded in December

  • Twenty-four-year-old Veronicah Chemutai passed away on Wednesday, July 21, after suffering birth complications
  • Chemutai died just a few months after getting married to her campus lover Victor Kuria
  • Kuria and Chemutai got married in December 2020; they fell in love when they were First Year students at Maasai Mara University

Family and friends in Subukia, Nakuru county are mourning the death of a 24-year-old woman who passed away on the evening of Wednesday, Thursday 21.

Veronicah Chemutai, who was a young vibrant woman died at the Bahati Level Three Hospital in Nakuru after suffering complications during birth; sadly, even the baby girl did not make it.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, a close friend of the family Myron Rotich said Veronicah's husband Victor Kuria was devastated but he was also holding up well.

"Kuria said 'she was my only world and I don't know what I will do after this'. But he is a man of character and he is pulling himself together as best as he can," Rotich said.

According to Kuria, his lovely wife was very assertive, never naive and knew what she wanted in life.

Campus lovers

Veronica and Victor got married in a colourful wedding in December 2020 and were just relishing their first year in marriage and looking forward to raising children together when her death occurred.

They fell in love when they were both First Year students at Maasai Mara University in 2015. They both graduated with a Second Class Honors Upper Division Bachelor's Degree in Education Arts.

"They were lover birds and power couple in the whole of Maasai Mara University," the family friend said.

They were not only lovers but they also shared a deep friendship as they did business together while on campus. As Myra aptly put it, their love stood the test of time up until her death.

Couple dies three days apart

In a separate report, TUKO.co.ke also brought you the story of Stanslous Mubanga Musonda and his wife Barbra Muuka Ndulu who were looking forward to welcoming a baby to their family, but that was not to be.

In the would-be lovely story which turned out to have a sad ending, the couple from Ndola, Zambia died three days apart leaving behind a two-day-old baby.

Stanslous died on Friday, July 16, when his wife Barbra was in labour. Barbra died on the morning of July 19, not long after being informed of her husband's death, the two dying within a span of three days.
