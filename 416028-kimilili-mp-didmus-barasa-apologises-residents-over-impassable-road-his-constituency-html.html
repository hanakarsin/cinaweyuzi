Kimilili member of parliament (MP) Didmus Barasa has apologised to residents in his constituency over a road that has been rendered impassable by heavy rains.

The first-term Jubilee lawmaker shared photos of the deplorable state of Moi Girls-Kamution'g road and vowed to fix it.

In the photos, motorists could be seen stuck in the mud as they tried to navigate their way to their respective destinations.

"My apologies to my people who are using Moi Girls-Kamution'g road.The road has been rendered impassable due to the ongoing grading and construction. We endeavour to deploy more equipment and personnel to ensure the road work is completed in a record time," Barasa tweeted.

The lawmaker said he regretted the incident and advised his constituents to use alternative routes.

"We encourage those affected to use alternative routes in the meantime. We regret the inconveniences made by the unexpected heavy downpour this evening. I am so sorry," he added.

Barasa's post attracted attention of Kenyans:

Jayseh Wanjalah said:

"Never seen an MP apologise for the inconveniences made. This man has characters that someone can emulate."

Sammy Khisa suggested:

"This road should have been tarmacked instead of wasting funds recarpeting."

John Makona wrote:

"Apologies accepted. Kindly we are requesting for it be upgraded to the level of what you have done to Kimilili-Khwiroro road."
