Mt Kenya: Cracks Widen as Meru Leaders Rally Behind Governor Kiraitu to Succeed Uhuru

  • To all appearences, Mt Kenya could be headed for an ugly split
  • Those from the eastern side accused their fellows from the western of being selfish
  • Going forward, the easterners vowed to run independently

The Mt Kenya region is yet to come out of the mucky political scenes as the leaders differ on who to rally behind ahead of the 2022 General Election.

This time around, the presumed underdogs from the mountain's eastern side have shown forth their determination to compete in national politics independently.

However, the communities from the said faction are undecided on who to lead the side.

National Assembly speaker Austin Muturi is the Ambeere's favourite.

The Ameru announced to have rested on Meru governor Kiraitu Murungi whom they will be backing for the presidency in the next polls.

Soon after the endorsement, the governor declared the communities from the east would no longer be "taken for a ride" by their fellows.

"It is no longer a cat and mouse game. It is a cat and dog game. We are tired of the cat and dog politics. When the dog is important when you are hunting, it should be important when you are eating...We have supported Kibaki for 10 years, we have supported Uhuru for 10 years, let us not hear of another candidate coming from that side (Mt Kenya west)," said Kiraitu.

Kiraitu accused their counterparts from the west, mostly Agikuyu, of riding on them (the easterners) for their own expediency and benefit.

The governor insisted that the Meru would have their own "horse" to vie for the presidency despite being undermined by their counterparts.

Also speaking in Meru on Saturday, June 12, Senator Mithika Linturi of Meru stated the need of the Agikuyu to reciprocate the support accorded to them for many years.

He insisted the Mt Kenya east residents will not relent on seeking their position in the national politics.

"We have stood with you for a very long time, we are not children of a lesser God. We will not be together. you will no longer confine us to your "ego". If the mountain is going to crack so be it," said Linturi.

Over the years, the mountain has edged over the other regions in the country due to the residents voting as a common bloc.

However, things tend to change as the respective politicians hold their ground on whom to support in the forthcoming campaigns.

This comes as Muturi, who also comes from the east, faces disapproval from a section of the Agikuyu leaders opposed to his installation as the mt Kenya region spokesperson.

Kirinyaga governor Anne Waiguru, Muranga's Mwangi was Iria, former Kiambu's William Kabogo are some of the leaders who expressed their dissatisfaction with Muturi's coronation.

We have the numbers

Citing their perceived high numbers, the Mt Kenya west politicians insisted they will still go for the top set despite having it for cumulative 35 years.

Speaking on Saturday, June 12, during a meeting at the Lusoi Lodge that brought together, governors, former legislators and aspirants, the politicians insisted theirs is a vote-rich region adding they will not accept to be pushed to the periphery in the next government.

"Benefits of a United region trickles down to its people. Being our second meeting, we consulted with leaders and other stakeholders from the Mt Kenya region to ensure our votes are secure and will count in 2022. We will continue reaching out to our people for a secured future," said Anne Waiguru.
