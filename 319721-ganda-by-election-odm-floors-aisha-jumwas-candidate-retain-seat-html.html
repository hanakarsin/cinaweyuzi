ODM whitewashes Aisha Jumwa's candidate in Ganda by-election

- Reuben Katana of Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) was declared winner after garnering 4, 177 votes

- His opponent, Abdul Omar, who was being supported by Malindi MP Aisha Jumwa came in second with 2, 331 votes

- Jubilee Party's candidate Joseph Kiponda came a distant third with 589 votes, followed by KADU Asili's David Mitsanze who garnered 362 votes

Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) candidate for Ganda ward by-election Reuben Katana has trounced his opponents including an opponent backed by the party's rebel MP Aisha Jumwa.

The win comes slightly over a day after chaos that left one person dead and Jumwa, the MP for Malindi arrested.

Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) declared Katana the winner on the night of Thursday, October 17, after he garnered 4,177 votes beating closest rival Abdul Omar who got 2,331 votes.

Omar contested for the seat on an Independent ticket.

Jubilee Party's candidate Joseph Kiponda came a distant third with 589 votes, followed by KADU Asili's David Mitsanze who garnered 362 votes.

The Ganda ward by-election was a was turned into a vicious battleground pitting ODM leaders against Jumwa who was supporting Omar, the independent candidate.

The election was deemed a litmus test for Jumwa who dumped the Orange party and dived into a political duvet with Deputy President William Ruto.

As such, the contest was seen as a supremacy battle between Raila and Ruto ally's influence in the coastal region.

Ganda seat fell vacant after the High Court in Malindi nullified the election of Abdulrahman Mohamed Omar citing irregularities that marred the 2017 poll.

ODM also registered victory in, Abakaile ward by-election in Dadaab constituency, Garissa county.

Mohamed Sheikh alias Amin Comp retained his seat after garnering 1,953 votes against Shexale who got 1,812 votes.

It was a narrow win where the victor and closest rival were separated by a margin of 141 votes.
