MP Babu Owino accuses govt of extorting, blackmailing legally operating betting firms

- The lawmaker argued the government's move to block paybill numbers of betting firms was unconstitutional

- He said the role of controlling betting firms was under county governments

- The legislator noted interference with the betting firms risked rendering many youths jobless

Embakasi East MP Paul Ongili alias Babu Owino has weighed in on the standoff between betting firms and the Betting Control and Licensing Board (BCLB) saying the government was blackmailing and extorting legally operating businesses.

The lawmaker observed the government's directive on mobile money transfer operators to suspend paybill numbers for blacklisted betting firms was unconstitutional.

In a Saturday, July 13, Instagram post, the MP argued that the move was discriminatory against giant gambling companies and could not stand the test of judicial scrutiny.

"It is clear that this directive is discriminatory and illegal and cannot stand the test of judicial scrutiny. Therefore, we must ask ourselves what is the motive behind this if not the institutional extortion and blackmail of legally operating businesses," said Babu.

The legislator argued that the function of controlling betting firms was under the devolved units as by the County Government Act and Constitution of Kenya.

"The national government is therefore exceeding its mandate in attempting to govern the operation of the betting industry. The Attorney General Paul Kihara needs to advise his colleagues accordingly," he said.

The youthful leader said the move risked rendering several Kenyan youths jobless as the companies have employed quite a large number of them in running their operations in the country.

"This draconian action will leave so many young people jobless at the end of month and cut off tens of thousands from livelihoods. Who will pay their children’s school fees? Who will feed these families?" he posed.

Already, 27 companies have been blacklisted and their paybill numbers blocked following government's directive.

Among those affected include Sportpesa, Betin, Betway, Betpawa, Elitebet and PremierBet among many others.

According to BCLB boss Liti Wambua, the affected companies allegedly failed to prove they were tax compliant.
