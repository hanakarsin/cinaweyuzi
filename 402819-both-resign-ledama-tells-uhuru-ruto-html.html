Both of you should resign, Ledama tells Uhuru, Ruto - Narok Senator Ledama Ole Kina has challenged P

- President Uhuru Kenyatta asked his deputy William Ruto to resign for perpetually undermining his government

- Narok Senator Ledama Ole Kina expressed disappointment in Uhuru for making the utterances in public and asked him to resign together with the DP

- Ledama also asked the youth to refuse to be used by politicians for their own selfish gains

Narok Senator Ledama Ole Kina has challenged President Uhuru Kenyatta to also prepare to resign after the head of state asked his deputy William Ruto to step aside for undermining his government.

Ledama, while reacting to the president's ferocious verbal salvo directed at Ruto, said both leaders should resign from government.

In a tweet he posted on Friday, Thursday 11, moments after Uhuru tore into his deputy, the vocal ODM senator asked the two senior politicians to stop fighting and embarrassing each other in public and instead resign and pave way for new leadership.

"Both of you should resign! Acheni kuvuana nguo nje! (stope undres*ing each other in public)," he tweeted.

Ledama also asked the youth to refuse to be used by politicians for their own selfish gains.

While speaking to students of Catholic University of Eastern Africa during the 2021 cultural week, the Narok legislator said it was utterly wrong for the youth to sit and watch old politicians purport to be speaking on their behalf without even listening to their voices.

"Sometimes we sitting in parliament and making noise about the youth, but it is us who are older who are speaking about what we want the youth to have but we are not giving you an opportunity to speak your language and I think it is about time you said no, if you are going to represent me, this is how II want to be represented not you making all these decisions for me," he said.

He further cautioned the students against being divided along ethnic and political lines sponsored by senior politicians in the country.

Havi's take

Law Society of Kenya (LSK) President Nelson Havi also challenged the president to step down if he had lost confidence in his deputy.

The advocate of the High Court argued Uhuru and Ruto were elected jointly as president and deputy president and were constitutionally inseparable.

"Let the President read the Constitution. The two of them were elected together: they came together, they can only resign together," he said via his Twitter handle.

Uhuru castigated Ruto during a public address at Uthiru market in Kiambu county accusing the DP of criticising and at the same time taking credit for Jubilee government's failure.

The infiltrated head of state challenged Ruto to resign if he was unhappy with the manner the government was being run.
