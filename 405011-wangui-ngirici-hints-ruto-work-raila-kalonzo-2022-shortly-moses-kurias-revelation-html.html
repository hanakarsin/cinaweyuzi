Kirinyaga woman representative Wangui Ngirici has hinted that Deputy President William Ruto, ODM leader Raila Odinga and Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka may work together ahead of the 2022 General Election.

The same sentiments had earlier been shared by Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria and the Opposition chief's elder brother Oburu Odinga who affirmed Raila will form an alliance when the right times comes.

Taking to Facebook on Sunday, March 7, Wangui used a coded language to describe the suspected alliance saying it is possible for wheelbarrows to carry oranges and watermelons.

Politics is very Dynamic. It's very possible for the wheelbarrow to carry oranges and water melons. This is the most common arithmetic in our markets, let us embrace political tolerance," said Ngirici.

In decoding her message, the second in command has been dishing out wheelbarrows while empowering 'hustlers' whom he banks on their votes to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta.

Raila's ODM party uses an orange as its logo while Kalonzo has been christened a watermelon due to his constant change in political stands and decisions.

Ruto's unassailable lead

Even though Raila and Ruto have traded barbs over the failures of the Jubilee government in the recent past, Kuria opined they will join forces again having been in one team before.

Speaking to online influencer Gerald Bitok, the Tangatanga allied MP claimed an alliance between the duo is in the offing and will be made public once it is finalised.

The MP said the former premier had decided to work with the DP after realising he had an "unassailable" lead in the 2022 presidential race.

In December 2020, Raila opened up on the possibility of working with the second in command saying they could have a handshake of their own if circumstances conspire.
