Uhuru in US for Bilateral Talks with President Joe Biden at White House

  • A statement by White House Press Secretary Jen Psaki indicated the two leaders will discuss the solid US-Kenyan bilateral relationship
  • They will also seek ways to build on the leaders' phone call in February 2021 and Biden's commitment to the US partnership with Africa
  • On Tuesday, October 12, Uhuru chaired a UN Security Council session on diversity, state-building, and peace

US President Joe Biden is set to hold his first one-on-one talks with an African leader on Thursday, October 14, when he hosts President Uhuru Kenyatta at White House.

According to a statement by White House Press Secretary Jen Psaki, the two heads of state will discuss the solid US-Kenyan bilateral relationship and the need to bring transparency and accountability to be domestic and international financial systems.

''Uhuru and his host will also discuss efforts to defend democracy and human rights, advance peace and security, accelerate economic growth, and tackle climate change,'' the statement seen by TUKO.co.ke read in part.

Uhuru, who chaired the Summit of the East Africa Community (EAC) Heads of State Summit, arrived in New York on Sunday, October 10, for a two-day official visit to the U.S.

UN Security Council session

On Tuesday, October 12, Uhuru chaired a UN Security Council session on diversity, state-building, and peace.

Having assumed her position as a non-permanent member of the UN Security Council on January 1, 2021, for two years, Kenya currently holds the monthly rotational presidency of the Council.

On Saturday, October 2, Uhuru assured United Nations member states of peace after Kenya was elected a non-permanent member of the U.N. Security Council.

Kenya defeated Djibouti for a seat designated for African countries.

Nine months ago, Kenya joined the United Nations Security Council.

The body is the principal global organ mandated to maintain and promote peace and security.

''I am delighted to inform you that the nation, in October 2021, will achieve the highlight of our two-year tenure on the Security Council by presiding over the council's affairs,'' Uhuru said.

Uhuru in talks with UN boss

On Monday, October 11, Uhuru held bilateral talks with the United Nations secretary-general, Antonio Guterres, at the UN headquarters in New York.

The two leaders discussed several subjects of global importance, including the crises in Haiti and Ethiopia's Tigray region and preparations for the forthcoming COP-26 meeting in Glasgow, Scotland.
