Residents of Eastleigh cry over increase in prostitution

- Residents of Eastleigh estate in Nairobi have decried the increase in prostitution in the area

- TUKO.co.ke spoke to several residents of the bustling estate on how the vice has affected their community

Eastleigh is a commercial hub at the heart of Nairobi, Kenya's capital city, the bustling estate is mostly inhabited by the Somali community who have invested in business and real estate.

Despite its economic growth, Eastleigh has been faced by a vice that is threatening its very existence and has plunged the communities living in the estate into a crisis.

Residents of Eastleigh have complained of an increase in the number of commercial sex workers in the estate.

The residents have complained that the prostitutes in the estate are eroding the morals of the youths in the area.

According to Sheikh Abdulahi Shariff, a religious leader who is the imam of the 12th street mosque, the commercial sex workers have eroded the moral fabric of the youth.

Shariff said the sex workers are posing not just spiritual danger to them but also risk infecting the youth with diseases like HIV/AIDS and other sexually transmitted diseases.

TUKO.co.ke spoke to one of the sex workers based at 12th street, the worker who we will not name says she lives in Dandora but has to ply the trade in Eastleigh as the job is profitable in the estate.

The sex worker confessed that the local community especially businessmen and the youth in the area are their major customers.

"They pay as well for our services and rarely bargain," she said.

According to Douglas Junior a resident of Eastleigh, for as little as KSh 200 one can be serviced by the low end sex workers lining the streets of Eastleigh. The price can triple for the high end sex workers operating in brothels that disguise themselves as lodgings in the bustling estate.

The desperate residents have now called on the government and relevant authorities to take action and remove the sex workers from the area.
