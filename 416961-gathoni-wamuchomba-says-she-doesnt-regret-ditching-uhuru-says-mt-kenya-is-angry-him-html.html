Gathoni Wamuchomba Says She Doesn't Regret Ditching Uhuru, Insists Mt Kenya Is Angry with Him

  • Gathoni Wamuchomba said President Uhuru's Mt Kenya's backyard is angry with him for his handshake deal
  • Wamuchomba also faulted Uhuru for the Jubilee-ODM coalition saying members were not consulted in the making of such a monumental decision
  • She said she does not regret joining Ruto's Tanga Tanga faction because the DP does not sideline them

Kiambu Woman Representative Gathoni Wamuchomba has spoken of her shift of allegiance from President Uhuru Kenyatta's Kieleweke to the rival Tanga Tanga faction.

The vocal politician ditched Uhuru and joined forces with his politically estranged deputy weeks after hinting that she was taking the detour.

Speaking on Sunday, June 27, Wamuchomba said she does not regret joining Tanga Tanga adding that her decision was occasioned by her constituents who told her that they are on Ruto's side.

Angry Mountain

The former radio presenter said Uhuru's Mt Kenya's backyard is angry with him because he did not involve them in his handshake deal with Opposition leader Raila Odinga.

"I do not know whether he is aware that the people are angry at him. They are angry because he did not involve them in his handshake deal," she said.

She further faulted the president for the impending Jubilee-ODM coalition saying Jubilee members were sidelined in the making of such a monumental decision.

"I just wonder, why can't they consult me? Or ask me my opinion? This is humiliation and we would rather support someone who hears us out," she said alluding she does not regret joining Ruto.

Joining Tanga Tanga

On Wednesday, June 23, pictures of the lawmaker and the deputy president revealed her change of heart.

She said the move to join Tanga Tanga was okayed by the electorate in Kiambu county. The legislator was welcomed by Ruto's loyalists who termed the move as a win for their team.

Wamuchomba joined Murang'a senator Irungu Kang'ata who earlier in the year ditched Uhuru's side for Ruto's.

A month before, she had posted a cryptic message which left many Kenyans guessing especially because she seemed to jubilate over the nullification of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

"I'm here 'behind the tent' and they say they do not want anger and threats. Why are people here, behind the tent, celebrating over the judgement?" she posed.
