Botswana President Ian Khama pens passionate open letter to Zimbabwe's Mugabe

- Botswana President Khama has called on Mugabe to resign

- Khama wrote Mugabe an open letter urging him to step down as president

- Mugabe, 93, has been president for 37 years and shows no signs of leaving office soon

Botswana President Ian Khama has written embattled Zimbabwe's leader Robert Mugabe an open letter asking him to step down.

In his letter, Khama observed that he had chosen to breach the necessary communication protocol between two Heads of State to convey his message to Mugabe.

Khama stated that Mugabe should pay attention to the desires of Zimbabweans and and voluntarily surrender power.

He added that the general population of Zimbabwe have for quite a while been subjected to untold enduring because of poor administration under Mugabe, insisting that the 93-year old should vacate office to pave way for a fresh political dispensation.

"I therefore reiterate my appeal to you to honourably step aside, if you really care for them, as you profess," the letter said in part.

In recent days, Mugabe has come under immense pressure to step down from Zimbabweans, sparking protests in the capital Harare against his administration.

Pundits have argued that the move by Mugabe to dismiss Emmerson Mnangagwa, as Vice President two weeks back may have activated calls for his resignation.

The move was seen by many as making room for Mr Mugabe's wife, Grace to succeed him.
