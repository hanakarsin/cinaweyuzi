Catholic Joins Anglican Church in Banning Politicians from Politicking in Church

  • The bishops said that any politician who attended mass must behave like any other worshipper
  • They warned that actions shall be taken against politicians who shall go against the new order
  • The bishops have also termed the bitter fallout between Uhuru and Ruto as dangerous

Catholic bishops have joined other joined Anglican Church in banning politicians from politicking in church.

The bishops stated that any politician who attended mass must behave like any other worshipper.

"And all politicians shall attend church services just like other faithful.. and in special consideration given to politicians to propagate his or her political agenda nor address the congregation in our places of worship," they said.

They said that actions shall be taken against politicians who shall go against the new order.

At tha same time, the bishops also termed the bitter fallout between President Uhuru Kenyatta and his Deputy William Ruto as dangerous to citizens.

" The bad relation of our topmost leaders is dangerous for the prevailing peace and tranquillity in the country and cannot be taken lightly.

"We are deeply concerned that if this open disagreement...is taken up by their supporters, the trickle-down effect it could generate across the country will be dire even to contemplate," " they added.

They stated that the public exchanges witnessed between Uhuru and Ruto are dangerous for the country.

In a separates story TUKO.co.ke reported that the Anglican Church Archbishop Jackson Ole Sapit said he was unapologetic on his recent ban that sent shivers down the spines of the political class after prohibiting politicking in church.

On Sunday, September 12, Ole Sapit irked a number of politicians led by Amani National Congress leader Musalia Mudavadi who walked out of the church service after the cleric barred them from speaking.

Speaking on Monday, September 13, the archbishop maintained that his decree remained unaltered, adding that all other churches should follow suit to restore sanity in the religious institutions.

"If all churches followed suit and banned politics in church, Kenya would be at a better place. We hope everybody is going to be on board on this.The moment we shy away on this issue the more the situation will worsen. We must be bold in protecting the pulpit," he said.

At the same time, Sapit also barred his bishops from allowing politicians to speak during church services adding that they taint the church's image by doing politics when given a chance to speak.
