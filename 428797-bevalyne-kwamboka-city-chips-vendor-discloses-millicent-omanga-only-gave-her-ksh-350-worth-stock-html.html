Bevalyne Kwamboka: Chips Vendor Discloses Millicent Omanga Only Gave Her KSh 350 Worth of Stock

  • Nominated senator Millicent Omanga announced her team had hooked up with a roadside chips vendor Bevalyne Kwamboka on October 4
  • Omanga disclosed she donated an umbrella, cash and even bought her a sack of Irish potatoes to keep her business going
  • The beautiful story of the campus student who had previously been heavily bashed for her dressing soon took a different twist

For the better part of Monday, October 4, Jubilee nominated senator Millicent Omanga became the talk of social media streets.

The Tanga Tanga Nairobi winger had just announced her benevolence to the world after her team hooked up with a roadside chips vendor Bevalyne Kwamboka, a young woman juggling school and business.

In her 25-worded social media post, which attracted hundreds of reactions, Omanga disclosed she donated an umbrella, cash and even bought her a sack of Irish potatoes to keep her business going.

"Bevalyne Kwamboka is now sorted. Umbrella, cash and stock to boost her hustle. Mvua au jua haitamharibia biashara tena. Now let's go eat those chips," she posted, stressing the young lady was now well-cared for.

But it did not end there. What was a beautiful story of a campus student who had previously been heavily bashed for her dressing took a different twist.

After the dust settled, three days later, Kwamboka shocked her followers including some well-wishers who may have wanted to help her, with her stunning revelation.

According to Kwamboka, the good senator donated a sack of potatoes worth KSh 350, one umbrella and two aprons as part of her generosity towards uplifting small scale business people.

She further alleged when the team visited her place of work, they removed umbrellas from their car and took a group photo before handing one to her (Kwamboka).

"For clarification, team Millicent Omanga only bought waru za 350, came out of the vehicle with five umbrellas, took photos, returned them and gave out one and two aprons. Hiyo mambo ya cash haikua plus stock ni ya 350. Hizo waru gunia kubwa I had bought and started the day," she clarified.

TUKO.co.ke tried to get a comment from Omanga on the issue.

However, the politician neither responded nor returned our calls.
