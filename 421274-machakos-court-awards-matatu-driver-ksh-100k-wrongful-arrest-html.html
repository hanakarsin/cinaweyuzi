The High Court has awarded a matatu driver KSh 100,000, in Machakos for wrongful arrest and prosecution.

Erastus Maina Karanja had been accused of obstructing county government officers and found guilty but appealed the verdict.

In his ruling, Justice David Kemei stated his rights were violated.

"The citizens expect the police officers in going about their duties to be fair, transparent and accountable in executing duties on behalf of the state," Kemei said as reported by Daily Nation.

Machakos county enforcement officers nabbed the driver on February 3, 2017.

Three offences

He was arraigned at a magistrate's court and charged with three offences; obstructing the officers, resisting impoundment of the vehicle and picking passengers at an undesignated area.

After the hearing and trial, he was acquitted of two counts but found guilty of obstructing the officers. As a result, he was ordered to pay a fine of KSh 5,000 or be sentenced to two months in prison.
