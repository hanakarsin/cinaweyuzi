Don't Succumb to Hormonal Urges if You Want Success, Equity CEO James Mwangi Advises Teens

  • Equity Group CEO James Mwangi argued that success goes in hand with self-control and the ability to discern between right and wrong
  • The CEO said many youths' destinies were ruined by indulgence in drugs and premarital affairs
  • According to him, self-control can be a challenging sacrifice but a worthy cause after all

Equity Group Holding chief executive officer James Mwangi has urged Kenyan youths to learn how to control their hormonal desires if they are zealous about succeeding in life.

The banker advised the youths to exercise self-control over their bodies and say no to urges that can destroy their future, saying every action taken today bears consequences.

Speaking on Wednesday, July 21, at Emurua Dikiir Secondary School in Narok county, Mwangi confided in the learners that the reason behind God's decision to human beings differ from animals was to give the former the ability to reason and choose what is best for them.

“When your body pushes you because of hormonal transformation, use your head to control your body. Otherwise you will engage in matters that could cause you regret later,” said Mwangi as quoted by KNA.

He stated that drug abuse and premarital affairs were taking a toll on the young people destroying their promising future.

He noted that many had lost their destinies to early and unplanned pregnancies and marriages.

“We are losing a lot of children, especially boys, because of drugs and early marriages that do not benefit them. I call upon young people to be focused because they are the future of our country,” he added.

He referred his talk to a 2014 report in which Narok was ranked as the leading county nationally in early pregnancies.

The report was produced by the Kenya Demographic Health Survey (KDHS), which ranked the county at 40 per cent, way above the national average of 18 per cent.

Mwangi was in the school to commission the 'Wings to Fly' scholarships from which 108 bright students from poor backgrounds are set to benefit.

In Emurua Dikirr Constituency, the beneficiaries of the programme were 24 students, 10 boys and 14 girls, under Wings to Fly and another 84 students, 46 girls and 38 boys, under Elimu Scholarship.
